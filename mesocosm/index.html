Introduction

What is a mesocosm and what are they used for?

A mesocosm is an enclosed enviornment that is commonly used in laboratories to investigate how parts of ecosystem interact with one another (Odum). Multiple mesocosms can be used simultaneously in order to see how individual parts interact with the ecosystem. One example of these individually-observed parts is chemicals in freshwater (Crossland & La Point).

What elements are needed to create a sustainable mesocosm?

Since an ecosystem usually cannot sustain itself without producers, they are necesscary for a sustainable mesocosm. H2O (water), sunlight, and CO2 are essential for the plant to perform photosynthesis (Crossland & La Point). Consumers are not needed to provide CO2 to producers since producers can use CO2 for aerobic respiration. Water does not need to be added since the O2 (oxygen) from photosynthesis will bond with the hydrogen in the jar to form more water to be recycled.

Water, oxygen, and the necessary vitamins and minerals are essential for consumers (Lavelle). Since Oxygen and water is already in the mesocosm, consumers can be added if the necessary vitamins and minerals are present.

Hypothesis

If my mesocosm has water, sunlight, and a plant, it will be able to sustain itself because the plant will be able to survive in that enviornment. If I add a non-producer and nutrient-rich elements to the mesocosm, the mesocosm will still be able to sustain itself because both the plant and the consumer will be able to sustain themselves.

Variables

The amount of sunlight, water, nutrients the soil, and CO2 will be independent variables. The organism's growth and survival will be the dependent variables.

Design

Original Design that Lasted Less Than a Day (Soda Bottle / First Attempt)

I filled a 2000 cm3 water bottle with about 3 to 4 'puffy' dandelion heads (Taraxacum officinale), about 500 cm3 of soil, an unmeasured amount of water, and about four earthworms (Lumbricus terrestris). I chose these materials under the assumption that the producer, or the dandelion seeds would grow and still be able to provide the worms oxygen. The majority of worms were not able to survive in these conditions and ended up surfacing by the first day.

It is likely that the dandelions would have grown eventually since they did have water, sunlight, and CO2. I figured it would not be ethical and wait a little longer to see if the dandelions would grow while the earthworms are still in the bottle, suffocating.

Since the producer in this ecosystem is already living, it will be able to produce oxygen in the ecosystem from the beginning, meaning the earthworm is more likely to live.

Disscussion & Conclusion

Conclusion

The hypothesis is partially supported by the data. The plant appeared to survive in an enviornment with sunlight and water; however, it could not be determined whether or not the worm was able to survive with water, nutrients, and oxygen. It is uncertain whether or not the plant survived completely since there were some dying ferns appearent by the end of the experiment. The precieved survival of the moss could be attributed to the water and sunlight present since the amount of CO2 could not be measured. Given that the plant did survive to an extent, the outcome was partially by research since it was able to survive with the resources used.

Limitations

My data collection methods were limited since I did not measure information such as the temperature of the jar and the initial amount of water I put into the jar, what nutrients were actually found in the soil, etc. This may have affected my experiment because it may have provided me with information that explains the moss's color changes. This could be avoided by making sure to have materials or methods for these measurements before the experiment takes place. In the future, I may stick a thermometer inside the mesocosm, measure the water before and after the experiment, and consider purchasing a nutrient analysis kit to use before and after the experiment.

Another limitation was my inability to see whether or not the worm was still present. This affected my experiment because it prevented me from actually figuring out whether or not my mesocosm was sustainable for non-producer organisms. this limitation could be avoided by reserving an area for the worm to live in or use a smaller container. By giving the worm a smaller space, it will be easier to determine whether or not it is living.

I was limited to experimenting with one kind of ecosystem. This has little affect on the current experiment, but it prevents me from knowing how sustainable other ecosystems are in mesocosms. I could avoid this by using different abiotic and biotic factors in future mesocosm experiments.

The jar's shape was a limitation since it did make the moss difficult to see sometimes. This is fairly important since it directly concerns qualitative plant data. To avoid this in the future, I would use a container that did not limit what could be seen clearly.
