Mia's Dress Blog

I'm making a dress for spirit week, and I needed somewhere to post the process.


9 • 8 • 19


When my father came home from grocery shopping, he came home with a small box of one-serving milk cartons. I asked for the box so I could make the ears.

I later went with my mother to store called JOANN's Fabrics and bought the materials:

  • Cloth
  • Interfacing
  • Felt
  • Pattern
  • Sticky Felt
  • Thread
  • Zipper



    • 9 • 20 • 19


    My grandmother came over to help my mom and I make the dress. They helped me measure the pattern in order to cut out the right size.

    They then taught me how to use the sewing machine.

    Using the sewing machine, I attached the cut-up pieces while my grandmother and mother helped with smaller parts such as ironing on the interfacing. Interfacing is the material that goes around the neck in order to keep the collar of the dress stiffer.

    We also made a purse with the remaining fabric



    9 • 23 • 19


    Two circles were traced into the cardboard with a circular plastic cup. Using a hole punch, I traced and punched out the geometrical shapes into the red and yellow sticky felt. I later stuck the felt onto the cardboard and traced crescents onto the circles using a cheap headband. My mother suggested that I should use some of the remaining felt on the headband. The crescents were cut out of the circles and stuck to the headbad using fabric glue.


    9 • 29 • 19


    Using the remaining yellow felt as well as the pink felts, I traced small and large circles into the felt with a pen and various round objects. My mother then helped me glue the felt on the dress and purse. We never actually used the zipper, so my mother decided to secure parts of the back with a series of hooks and eyes.
